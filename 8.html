First Generation (1940 -56 ) : Vacuum Tubes.


First Generation computers were Vacuum Tubes/thermionic valves based machines.These computers used vacuum tubes for circuitary and magnetic drums for memory.A magnetic drum is a metallic cylinder coated with magnetic iron - oxide materiall on which data and programs can be stored.Input was based on punched cards and paper tape and output was displayed on printouts

First Generation computers relied on binary-coded language( language of 0's and 1's ) to perform operations and were able to solve only one problem at a time.Each machine wasfed with different binary codes and they were difficult to program.This resulted in lack of versatility and speed.I n addition,to run on different types of computers ,instructions must be written or recompiled.

Examples: ENIAC, EDVAC and UNIVAC are examples of First Generation Computers

Characteristics Of First Generation Computers:-

  • These computers are based on vacuum tube technology
  • Computation time was milliseconds
  • These compters were large and required a lot of space for installation
  • They generated large amount of heat
  • These were non-portable and Very slow equipments
  • They were expensive to operate and used large amount of electricity
