Fourth Generation (Elarly 1970s-Till Date): Microprocessors

The fourth generation is an extension of third generation technology.Although,the technology of this generation was still based on the integrated circuit,these have been made readily available to us because of the development of the microprocessor.The Intel 4004(10) chip,which was developed in 1971,took the integrated circuit on step further by locating all the components of computer on minuscule chip.A microprocessor is built onto a single piece of silicon,known as chip.It is about 0.5cm along one side and no more than 0.05cm thick,

Fourth generation computers became more powerfu,compact,reliable and affordable.As a result,it gave rise to personal computer(PC) revolution.This generation also saw the development of GUI(Graphical User Interface) mouse and hand held devices.Despite of all advantages,there is only one disadvantage of this generation,that is,this generation required complex and sophisticated technology for manufacturing CPU and other components.

Example: Apple II,Altair 8800 and CRAY-1

Characteristics Of Fourth Generation Computers:-

  • Fourth generations computers are microprocessor-based system.
  • Very small in Size
  • Cheapest among all other generations
  • They are Portable and very reliable
  • These machines generate negligible amount of heat,hence air conditioning is not required
  • Graphical User Interface and pointing devices enables users to learn to the computer quickly
